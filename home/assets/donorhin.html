About 3rd Challenge

Number of team members: 7

Duration: 3 months

Challenge: Helping community

This project started by discussing which communities that the team wanted to do research about. One of the team members knew someone in Rh- community. The team searched about the community and I contacted the community through phone numbers that was seen on the community's website. The team did a lot of research about blood donor transactions in Indonesian Red Cross and tried to understand how this community works. I empathised all of the interviewee by listening to their story, going to the nearest Red Cross place, asking about all of the obstacles that they faces, and gathering all the articles about Rh- donors deficiency.

In the first month, the app was called Rhelpus yet later it was changed into Donorhin since the pronunciation in Bahasa Indonesia for Rhelpus is unfamiliar. When Rhelpus was built, these are the 3 main important things:

Considering low percentage of bearers and blood type compatibility, rhesus negative bearers highly depend on each other that impacts their blood donation ecosystem to be exclusive. Illegal case that happens in blood donation is buying-and-selling blood. Since blood donating has a connection with health data, it is important to think about user privacy.

I helped in sketching Crazy 8's, wire-framing, making the app flow, creating some UI pages, and becoming UX writer. I have done some user testing in the design phase since one of the students in the academy is a family member of Rh- bearers. Together with my designer teammate, I did some iterations of the app colour and asset placement. At last, the team presented the solutions and got a positive feedback since the problems are in line with the needs and solutions.

In the second and third month, the team did some user testing to improve the app. The main problem with the app was how to find our target user since in Indonesia, there is no database for blood type. The team finally came up with a solution to co-operate with all the Rh- communities all over Indonesia by providing the coordinators to promote Rh- communities events. The formation of style guide was initiated and the UI/UX was changed due to re-branding. Around this time, the app was called Donorhin and the data structure was more complicated. The developer teammates studied design patterns and I read about CloudKit to put coordinates data of Indonesian Red Cross donor places there. Furthermore, I scheduled sprints as in agile method, tested the app manually for QA, and posted app-needed-improvement on Trello. After overcoming various hurdles, the team presented the final solution. As one of the presenters, I received comments that was astonished by the mission of the app.
